A Yorkshire Village

Situated perfectly in the heart of West Witton, a quaint village remained unchanged for decades, stands Loaning Cottage.

Directly across the road is the village shop serving up freshly baked bread from the village and a selection of pies, pasties and of course Yorkshire puddings. The shop is a great starting point to stock up on supplies for the day or treat yourself to some of the award-winning produce from the surrounding farms.

Plan the day ahead by a quick online visit to the Yorkshire website. Here you can find details of local walks through the country side and many of the local attractions. One such hike is to the top of Pen Hill which can be seen from the window of Loaning cottage.

This is a circular walk starting from Grassgill lane and returning by Chantry Bank. After a long walk you deserve a pint of traditional Yorkshire ale at the Fox & Hounds. You’ll be greeted with a warm welcome by the landlord Andrew ‘Blacky’ Blackburn and his wife Nola.

The pub serves up a delicious menu of authentic pub food and fine ales, it is listed in the CAMRA (Campaign for real ales) guide. You will also see impromptu music sessions and a quiz night every second Friday. Ask one of the friendly locals about the annual 'Burning of Bartle festival.

After a few refreshing pints the walk home is a mere 30 seconds.

North Yorkshire

Looking a little further afield the town of Leyburn is only a 5 minute drive away or for the more adventurous a 45 minute walk through the fields and follows the railway line into town. A local tourist office/café supplies more information about the area.

Visit the Forbidden Corner and the sister town of East Whitton. A little further down the road is the town of Masham home to the Black Sheep Brewery. Here you can take a tour and hear the fascinating story of how the brewery was established.

Drive the opposite way along Mesnes lane and you can find the Berry's farm shop and cafe, Aysgarth Falls and finally the market town of Hawes where you can join the locals for a cream tea in the Penny Garth Cafe.

Any trip to Hawes must include a visit to the world famous Wensleydale Creamery. Try a sample of over 70 cheeses and goes without saying to try Wensleydale with cranberries and a slice of fruit cake.

Back in West Witton an absolute gem of a restaurant awaits you in 'The Heifer'. Be sure to book yourself in for an evening meal. Serving up the very best of the local ingredients
